## 2. ACADEMIC STRUCTURE

### Degree Offered
- **Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (BSBA)**
- Duration: 4 years
- Total Credits Required: 130 semester credits
- Recognition: Government Decree n° 2019-162 of February 18, 2019

### Academic Calendar
- **System**: US Higher Education System with semesters
- **Structure**: 2 main semesters per year
  - Fall Semester
  - Spring Semester
- **Examination Periods**: Midterm and Final Examinations

### Academic Levels
1. **Freshman (1st Year)**: Credits 0-32
2. **Sophomore (2nd Year)**: Credits 32-66 (requires minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA)
3. **Junior (3rd Year)**: Credits 66-100 (requires minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA)
4. **Senior (4th Year)**: Credits 100+ (requires minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA)

### Credit System
- **System**: US Credit Hour System
- **Definition**: 1 credit hour = 1 hour of lecture per week for one term
- **Workload**: 
  - For 3-credit course: 42 contact hours + 84 hours home study + 21 hours tutoring
  - Each lecture hour requires approximately 2 hours of home study
- **Conversion**: 1 US credit = 14 contact hours

## 7. GRADING POLICY & GPA SYSTEM

### Grading Scale

| Letter Grade | Grade Point | Score (out of 100) | Score (out of 20) |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| A | 4.0 | > 90 | ≥ 16.00 |
| A- | 3.7 | 87-90 | 15.00-16.00 |
| B+ | 3.3 | 83-87 | 14.00-15.00 |
| B | 3.0 | 80-83 | 13.00-14.00 |
| B- | 2.7 | 77-80 | 12.00-13.00 |
| C+ | 2.3 | 73-77 | 11.00-12.00 |
| C | 2.0 | 70-73 | 10.00-11.00 |
| C- | 1.7 | 67-70 | 9.00-10.00 |
| D+ | 1.3 | 65-67 | 8.50-9.00 |
| D | 1.0 | 60-65 | 8.00-8.50 |
| F | 0.0 | < 60 | < 8.00 |

### Key GPA Requirements

| Requirement | GPA Threshold |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Minimum to Pass** | D (1.0) or higher in each course |
| **Degree Completion** | 2.0/4.0 cumulative |
| **Dean's Honor List** | 3.7 or higher (requires C or above in all courses) |
| **Graduation Honors - Cum Laude** | 3.40-3.59 |
| **Graduation Honors - Magna Cum Laude** | 3.60-3.79 |
| **Graduation Honors - Summa Cum Laude** | 3.80-4.00 |
| **Academic Probation** | Below 2.0 cumulative |
| **Satisfactory Progress** | Minimum semester GPA 2.0 + cumulative GPA 2.0 |

### Grade Point Average (GPA) Calculation
**Formula**: GPA = Sum of (Grade Value × Credits) / Total Credits

**Example:**
- Course W (3 credits): Grade B = 3.0 × 3 = 9.0
- Course X (3 credits): Grade A- = 3.7 × 3 = 11.1
- Course Y (3 credits): Grade D = 1.0 × 3 = 3.0
- Course Z (1 credit): Grade B+ = 3.3 × 1 = 3.3
- **GPA = (9.0 + 11.1 + 3.0 + 3.3) / 10 = 2.64**

### Requirements to Progress Between Levels

| Level Transition | Earned Credits Required | Cumulative GPA Required | Additional Requirements |
|------------------|------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Freshman → Sophomore** | All 100-level courses (32 credits) | 2.0 or higher | - |
| **Sophomore → Junior** | All 100 & 200-level courses (66 credits) | 2.0 or higher | Can select Major/Minor only if specialized GPA also 2.0+ |
| **Junior → Senior** | All 100, 200 & 300-level courses (100 credits) | 2.0 or higher | - |
| **To Graduation** | All 130 required credits | 2.0 or higher | Fulfill major, minor, and all other requirements |

### Satisfactory Academic Progress Standards
Students must maintain:
1. Regular class attendance
2. Minimum semester GPA of 2.0
3. Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0

### Course Repetition Policies

**Forgiveness Policy:**
- Students may repeat maximum 4 courses with grades below "C"
- Can use forgiveness policy only ONCE per course
- Both grades appear on transcript, but only highest counts toward GPA
- NOT eligible for courses failed due to academic misconduct
- Student must request forgiveness (not automatic)

**Failing Grades:**
- F grade permanently remains on transcript
- Student MUST retake the course
- Can use forgiveness policy unless F was for academic dishonesty

**Course Transfer:**
- Transfer credits awarded for grades of "C" or higher
- Grades from other institutions NOT calculated in TBS GPA
- Only courses integrating into TBS program can transfer

## 15. STUDENT CODE OF CONDUCT & RULES

### Class Attendance Policy
- **Attendance Expectation**: Students must attend all classes regularly
- **Absence Limit**: Maximum 4 absences per semester per course
- **Consequence**: Beyond 4 absences, student not permitted to take final exam
- **Exam Absence**: Any absence during exam = grade of 0
- **Definition**: 1 absence = 3-hour class session
- **Tardiness**: May negatively impact grades at instructor's discretion

### Academic Integrity

**Plagiarism (Strictly Prohibited):**
1. **Plagiarism of Ideas**: Using author's ideas without credit/referencing
2. **Plagiarism of Words**: Using phrases/sentences without quotation marks or proper citation
3. **Plagiarism of Ideas and Words**: Combining unauthorized ideas and words

**Cheating (Strictly Prohibited):**
- Definition: Fraudulent and/or deceptive acts for unearned academic advantage
- Typically occurs during examinations
- TBS takes most serious view of such offences

**Penalties:**
- Failing grade for the course
- Suspension from school
- Cannot use forgiveness policy for grades due to academic dishonesty

### Work Habits Standards
Students expected to:
- Be prepared and participate in each class
- Possess necessary class materials
- Complete classwork and homework accurately and on time
- Be adequately prepared for quizzes, tests, and examinations

### Teamwork Standards
- Define roles within group (facilitator, leader, summarizer, evaluator, mediator, recorder)
- Demonstrate openness to new ideas and diverse viewpoints
- View teammates as collaborators, not competitors
- Respectfully listen and manage conflict
- Show high sense of cohesion and support

### Examination Rules
- **Exam Periods**: Midterm and final examinations
- **Schedule**: Set by administration at beginning of academic year
- **Travel Restriction**: Students advised against travel before exam dates
- **Absence**: Any absence during exam = grade of 0

### Respect & Conduct Requirements

**Respect for Self and Others:**
- Be honest and behave with dignity
- Treat others with respect and courtesy
- Use appropriate language, actions, and attire
- Prohibited: Verbal or physical harassment

**Respect for Authority:**
- Comply with all school rules
- Obey all laws
- Respond respectfully to all adults
- Proper address: "Ma'am" (ladies), "Sir" (gentlemen)

**Respect for Property:**
- Treat school and others' property with care
- Keep restrooms clean
- Use allocated rubbish bins for garbage
- **STRICT NO SMOKING POLICY**: Campus is smoke-free

## 21. KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR CAREER ADVISING

### For TBS Students:

1. **Early GPA Management**: Maintain 2.0+ GPA from start - critical for major selection in sophomore year
2. **Specialized GPA**: Track specific courses required for major GPA (varies by major)
3. **Time-bound Decision**: Major selection must happen after earning 66 credits + meeting GPA requirements
4. **Internship Planning**: Decide between Option I (12-credit internship) or Option II (6-credit project + electives) during junior year
5. **Elective Strategy**: Senior year allows customization through electives and minor selection
6. **Career Focus**: School's objective is "zero unemployment" - leverage career services and internship requirements
7. **Academic Integrity**: Forgiveness policy only for non-academic misconduct grades
8. **Attendance Matters**: Missing 5+ classes can result in exam exclusion
9. **Minor Flexibility**: Many minors have different course tracks based on major
10. **International Opportunities**: Consider partnerships and accreditation standards for global career prospects

### For Career Advisors:

1. **GPA Benchmarking**: 
   - 2.0-2.5: At risk for restrictions
   - 2.5-3.0: Adequate progress
   - 3.0-3.7: Good standing
   - 3.7+: Excellence (Dean's List eligible)

2. **Major Preparation Tracking**:
   - Monitor specialized GPA during freshman/sophomore years
   - Plan course sequences to optimize grades in major-specific prerequisites

3. **Internship Planning**:
   - Begin internship search during junior year
   - Help students choose Option I vs Option II based on career goals

4. **Remediation Support**:
   - For students with <2.0 GPA: Academic counseling immediately
   - Maximum 2 consecutive semesters on probation before dismissal

5. **Honors Track**:
   - Students targeting honors need 3.4+ GPA with discipline
   - No sanctions during program

6. **Course Load Planning**:
   - Freshman: 27-28 hours/week
   - Sophomore: 24-25 hours/week
   - Junior/Senior: 24-27 hours/week

7. **English Proficiency**:
   - All courses in English - ensure strong communication skills
   - NBC 100, 120, 200 are critical foundations
